M19 [piano lightning cadenza] is written into the structure of [A], [C], [D].
M19 [piano lightning cadenza] in [A] and [C] foreshadows the start of M19
[piano lightning cadenza] at the end of [D].

M10 [tremoli fantastiques] is written into the structure of [A] and [C]. M10
[tremoli fantastiques] follows immediately after M19 [piano lightning cadenza]
in both [A] and [C]. M19 [piano lightning cadenza] and M10 [tremoli
fantastiques] are bound together in the piece. the final appearance of M19
[piano lightning cadenza] in [D] and [E] is accompanied by M10 [tremoli
fantastiques] even though M10 [tremoli fantastiques] is not written into the
structure of either [D] or [E].

M4 [contrabass halo] appears in the piece for the last time in [C].

M3 [dialoguing wind solos] is written into the structure of [B], [C], [D]. M3
[dialoguing wind solos] in [B] undergoes a transformation into M9 [sostenuti
fantastiques]. in [C] there is no structural reason to transform M3 [dialoguing
wind solos] into another material.

M1 [upper-register prestissimo] is written into the structure of [C] and [E].
M1 [upper-register prestissimo] climaxes in [C]. M3 [dialoguing wind solos] and
M1 [upper-register prestissimo] alternate in [C] (exercising a type of
windowing technique or a type of alternation technique).

M2A [seaweed guiro] is written into the structure of [C] and [E]. M2A [seaweed
guiro] in [E] only glances passingly back at earlier parts of the piece. M2A
[seaweed guiro] brings M1 [upper-register prestissimo] to conclusion. M2A
[seaweed guiro] crosses over from [C] into [D].
